**Theorem 6.1 (Conway-Sloane, 1999)**:
The lattice constructed from the H(8,4) code via Construction A is isomorphic to the E8 lattice, the unique even unimodular lattice in 8 dimensions.

$$\Lambda(H_{8,4}) \cong E_8$$

### 7.2 Computational Complexity Limits from Gottesman-Knill Theorem

**Theorem 7.1 (Gottesman-Knill, 1998)**:
Quantum circuits consisting only of Clifford group (normalizer of Pauli group) and computational basis measurements can be efficiently simulated classically.

**Proof Outline**:
- Clifford operations map Pauli operators to Pauli operators
- Via stabilizer formalism, $n$-qubit states can be described with $O(n^2)$ bits
- Each Clifford operation can be classically simulated in $O(n^2)$ time

**Consequence**: MTC satisfaction alone **does not guarantee BQP completeness**.

### 7.3 Necessary and Sufficient Conditions for Universal Quantum Computation

**Theorem 7.2 (Solovay-Kitaev, 1995)**:
If a finitely generated group $G$ is dense in SU(2), then operations generated by $G$ can approximate any unitary operation in polynomial time.

**Corollary (Sufficient Condition for BQP Completeness)**:
Universal quantum computation is possible with the following two:

1. **Clifford Group** $\mathcal{C}_n$ (established in Part I)
2. **A single Non-Clifford gate** $U \notin \mathcal{C}_n$
